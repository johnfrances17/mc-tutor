APPLICATION DEVELOPMENT AND EMERGING TECHNOLOGY: FINAL
DOCUMENTATION FORMAT
Purpose:
This guide outlines the official structure and formatting standards for the final printed
documentation of the Application Development and Emerging Technologies course. It
ensures consistency, professionalism, and academic rigor across all submissions and
must accompany the system during the final defense.
I. GENERAL FORMATTING GUIDELINES
 Paper Size: A4
 Margins: 1 inch on all sides
 Font Style: Times New Roman
 Font Size: 12 pt (body text), 14 pt Bold (chapter titles)
 Line Spacing: 1.5
 Text Alignment: Justified
 Paragraph Indent: First line 0.5 inch
 Pagination: Bottom center, starting on Chapter 1
 Binding: Softbound (black cover) with a clear plastic front
II. DOCUMENT STRUCTURE
1. Cover Page:
o Project Title (uppercase)
o Names of Group Members (First Name MI. Last Name)
o Submitted to: College of Computer Studies, Mabini Colleges, Inc.
o Course: Bachelor of Science in Computer Science
o Course Code: Application Development and Emerging Technologies
o Instructor’s Name: Aeron Dave C. Enova
o Semester and School Year
2. Approval Sheet:
o Includes signature lines for Panel/Instructor
3. Acknowledgment:
o Recognition of individuals who assisted in the project
4. Abstract:
o 200-300 words summarizing the system's objectives, methods, results,
and conclusions
5. Table of Contents:
o Auto-generated with correct page numbers
6. List of Figures
7. List of Tables
CHAPTER I – INTRODUCTION
 Background of the Study
 Statement of the Problem
 Objectives of the Study (General and Specific)
 Scope and Delimitation
 Significance of the Study

 Definition of Terms (if applicable)
CHAPTER II – REVIEW OF RELATED LITERATURE AND STUDIES
 Local and Foreign Literature
 Local and Foreign Studies
 Synthesis
 Theoretical and Conceptual Framework
CHAPTER III – SYSTEM DESIGN AND METHODOLOGY
 System Overview
 Use Case Diagram
 Data Flow Diagram or Architecture Diagram
 Methodology (e.g., Agile, Waterfall, Spiral)
 Project Timeline (Gantt Chart)
 Hardware and Software Requirements
CHAPTER IV – SYSTEM IMPLEMENTATION AND RESULTS
 Description of System Features
 Screenshots with Captions and Descriptions
 Source Code Snippets (if necessary)
 Test Cases and Testing Results
 Bug Log and Fixes
CHAPTER V – SUMMARY, CONCLUSION, AND RECOMMENDATIONS
 Summary of Results
 Conclusions
 Recommendations (for improvement, future work, etc.)
